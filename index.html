Serpentarius Healing Centre

The name "Serpentarius" is derived from greek and roman mythology. Asclepius (Aesculapius) was the greco-roman god of healing, truth and prophecy, and the son of Apollo, the god of medicine.

It was said he learned the secrets of defying death when he observed a serpent heal another by treating it with herbs. He was so successful in the healing arts that Pluto had him killed because there were fewer mortals entering the underworld. Jupiter regretted this and placed Asclepius in the heavens as the constellation "Serpentarius".
